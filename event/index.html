Security is Complicated

Tools must withstand complex attacks from well-resourced adversaries, and defend against current and future vulnerabilities.

Digital security tools and practices are intricate and often fragile, with a focus on resisting ever more complex and increasing commoditized attacks. The playing field is not equal.

Threats are Real

For Human Rights Defenders, LGBTQI Activists, Journalists, and other vulnerable populations, they are a matter of life and death.

Failure to be secure has very real consequences for targeted and vulnerable populations. A breach in privacy can mean the loss of one's job, imprisonment, or much worse.

Safety needs to Scale

At-risk users need to be able to use and understand the tools - and share that knowledge with their community.

We have powerful tools which can stand up to these threats -- but it is no longer enough for a tool to be secure. I have to be able to learn it and share it with my colleagues and my community.

What is USABLE?


USABLE connects Human-Centered Design, Digital Security Training, and Tool Development

So what *is* USABLE? USABLE connects communities to tools which meet their needs through a digital security training by also including developers for that tool and design experts.

Empathy matters.

Building feedback loops between at-risk communities, developers, trainers, and designers leads to deeper understanding of the threat landscape and creates ongoing interaction.

USABLE connects communities around the world with leading UX experts and digital security tool developers to solve real problems, build better tools, and create lasting learnings and design guidance for others to incorporate.

Tool Feedback Trainings

We began with 5 Tool Feedback Trainings (TFT) with at-risk communities around the world.

So far we have done 5 of Thesee Tool Feedback Trainings, each with a different community facing different threats. We start by working with the community to discuss what they need, and connect them with a relevant tool developer.
