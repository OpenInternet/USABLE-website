USABLE

Usable Security Apps By Leveraging End Users

Next

Connecting Human-Centered Design, Digital Security Training, and Tool Development

USABLE convenes leading UX experts and tool developers with digital security training events for at-risk communities around the world to solve real problems, build better tools, and create lasting, re-usable user personas for others to incorporate.

After bringing these stakeholders together for an intense 4-day UXForum, USABLE launches the UXFund to target small grants to user-driven usability and accessibility improvements for digital security tools.

Next

Unlocking Scale

Digital security tools have evolved in response to the multiple, varied challenges that face human rights defenders in extreme environments. This has built tools amazingly resistant to attacks; with significant amounts of ongoing funding and effort going towards maintaining their viability. Having these technical solutions alone is no longer sufficient. They must be easy and simple to understand, welcoming users from diverse backgrounds and technical skill levels. Any tool, no matter how secure, leaves a user insecure if left unused, or if used insecurely.

Adding a strong usability component to digital security tools reduces the risk of human error, but more importantly it unlocks the potential of real scale. As privacy-protecting tools become ubiquitous, they benefit from network effects, look less suspicious, and better resist negative policy changes. Usable tools providing real security and privacy at scale renew the promise of technology in a world where even governments are investing in typewriter ribbons.
